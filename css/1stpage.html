CARROTS

Root Vegetable

The carrot (Daucus carota) is a root vegetable often claimed to be the perfect health food. It is crunchy, tasty, and highly nutritious. Carrots are a particularly good source of beta carotene, fiber, vitamin K1, potassium, and antioxidants

Like all root plants, carrots grow best in cool weather climates, between 60°F and 70°F. If the climate is too hot, the carrots might not grow as large, and might have an overpoweringly strong flavor.

WHEAT

Wheat is the main cereal crop in India.

Whole wheat is a source of several vitamins and minerals, including selenium, manganese, phosphorus, copper and folate.

Wheat crop has wide adaptability. Wheat cultivation requires cool climate and medium rainfall. The ideal temperature needed in winters for the production of this crop is 10°C to 15°C. The ideal temperature needed in summers for the production of this crop is 21°C to 26°C.

BACK

SUGARCANE

Belongs to the grass family

Sugarcane is not a fruit nor a vegetable. It is a type of perennial grass like bamboo. Sugarcane stores its sugar in the stems (aka the canes), which makes them sweet. Sugarcane is a natural source of sucrose that is the powerhouse of our energy.

Sugarcane is a tropical plant, therefore, requires a year warm weather to reach maturity. The areas having temperature of 20° to 26°C and an average rainfall of 150 cm are suitable for its cultivation.

BACK

CORN

Also known as Maize and is a cereal

Corn has several health benefits. Because of the high fiber content, it can aid with digestion. It also contains valuable B vitamins, which are important for overall health.

A temperature of 21°C- 27°C and 50-100cm rainfall is suitable for its cultivation. It grows well in alluvial and red soil.

BACK

COCONUT

Coconuts are high in manganese, which is essential for bone health and the metabolism of carbohydrates, proteins, and cholesterol. They're also rich in copper and iron, which help form red blood cells, as well as selenium, an important antioxidant.

Coconut requires an equatorial climate with high humidity. The ideal mean annual temperature is 27°C with 5-7°C diurnal variation.

BACK

ONION

Onion is neither a root nor a stem. It is a tunicate bulb having a cluster of fleshy leaves. An onion is a modified underground stem structure. Onions are good sources of vitamin C, vitamin B6, potassium and folate

Onion grows under a wide range of climatic conditions but mild season without great extremes of heat or cold or excessive rainfall is best suited. It requires a temperature of 12-25°C.

BACK

RICE

Rice is one of the chief grains of India

India is the world's second largest producer of rice, and the largest exporter of rice in the world.

Rice is mainly grown in rain-fed areas that receive heavy annual rainfall. It demands temperature of around 25°C and above, and rainfall of more than 100 cm. Rice is also grown through irrigation in those areas that receive comparatively less rainfall.
